community entries

Service-learning and volunteerism are vital components of all academic disciplines in the university. Students are challenged to test theories and principles through actual community work. With identified partner communities, Silliman students develop projects and programs, integrating expertise in business development, health care, legal management, and environmental advocacy.

Climate Talks: Just Transition

On November 4, I had the opportunity to attend the Climate Talks seminar. The event featured three experts who shared their various experiences and knowledge about the issue of Climate Change as well as gave insights regarding on the Just Transition.

As someone who is passionate about the environment, I was excited to learn more about just transition and how it relates to climate change. The speakers did a great job of explaining the concept and providing examples of how it has been implemented in different parts of the world. They also discussed the challenges and opportunities associated with just transition and how it can help mitigate the impacts of climate change. It was a really informative and thought-provoking seminar, and I left feeling more aware and empowered to take action on this important issue.

Overall, it was a great experience to attend the seminar and learn from the experts about just transition and climate change. I was able to learn additional information about making effective use of my waste by recycling it, as well as other techniques.

Walk for a Creation

I recently had the chance to attend a walk for creation event hosted by the Diocese of Dumaguete on October 21. The event was held to bring awareness to our environment and connect faith with the environment. As someone who values both my faith and the environment, I was excited to participate in the walk and learn more about how these two things intersect.

The event was well organized and featured a variety of speakers and activities. The message of the walk was powerful and resonated with me. It was a great reminder of the importance of taking care of the earth and how our faith can motivate us to do so.

Overall, it was a meaningful and enriching experience to attend the walk for creation event and learn about the connection between faith and the environment.

Christmas Tree Volunteerism

I had the opportunity to participate in a Christmas-making volunteerism event last November 27 where I had to paint on a canvas based on a prompt. Although I am not an artistic person, working with my group of friends made the experience enjoyable.

We were able to collaborate and come up with a unique and creative design for our canvas. It was great to see how our different ideas and styles came together to form a cohesive piece. As we painted, we also had the opportunity to represent our organization, RYLF. In the end, we were able to successfully complete the prompts and contribute not only to the event but also to our organization.

Overall, it was a fun and fulfilling experience to participate in the Christmas-making volunteerism event and work with my friends to create something special.

SUROTC Fundraising Food Kiosk

On November 20, I had the chance to support a fundraiser hosted by the SU ROTC unit. The fundraiser was held to raise funds for the Dumaguete city jail, and the ROTC unit decided to sell food as a way to generate income. As someone who values giving back to the community, I was excited to support the fundraiser and contribute to a good cause.

I decided to purchase some food and was impressed by the quality and taste. The ROTC unit had clearly put a lot of effort into preparing the dishes and it showed in the final product. As I ate, I also had the opportunity to chat with some of the members of the ROTC unit and learn more about their motivations for hosting the fundraiser.

Overall, it was a great experience to support the fundraiser hosted by the SU ROTC unit. Not only did I have the chance to contribute to a good cause, but I also had the opportunity to enjoy some delicious food and learn more about the ROTC unit and their efforts to help the community. It was a win-win situation and I was glad to be able to support such a worthy cause.
