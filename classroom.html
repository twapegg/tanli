classroom entries

The classroom is a major component of the “second home” of students. Teaching at Silliman is made to challenge students to question prevailing concepts and theories, and find meaning in them in their day to day activities. Important to Silliman University is a mentoring system that walks students through the learning process of life.

Balik Talent Lecture Series

On August 23, I had the opportunity to attend a lecture series given by Dr. John Boaz Lee, an alumnus of Silliman who now works at Facebook. The lecture focused on the topic of jobs in IT and emphasized on the students who are interested in pursuing a career in the field of IT.

Dr. Lee is a specialist in the field of machine-learning and conducts research at Facebook. He shared his insights and experiences on how a PhD can open up doors to exciting career opportunities in academia and industry. He emphasized the importance of choosing the right research area and supervisor, and gave tips on how to write a successful PhD proposal.

Overall, it was a fascinating and informative lecture series. I'm grateful to have had the opportunity to attend and I'm considering pursuing a PhD in the future. Thank you to Dr. John Boaz Lee for sharing his wisdom and expertise with us.

Guidance and Counseling

On August 23, I had the opportunity to attend a guidance and counseling session where the counselor assessed our overall experience and mental health during our period of studying in the pandemic. It was a valuable and necessary session, as the pandemic has brought many challenges and stressors that have affected our well-being.

During the session, the counselor listened to our concerns and provided us with insights and strategies on how to better prepare ourselves for the change to hybrid learning mode, where face-to-face sessions will now be implemented. She emphasized the importance of self-care and communication, and gave us tips on how to manage our time and emotions effectively.

Overall, it was a helpful and supportive session. I'm grateful to have had the opportunity to attend and I'm looking forward to implementing the strategies and techniques that I learned to better manage my mental health and prepare myself for the new adaptation.

Industry Lecture Series

Attending the Industry Lecture on August 23 at the ATA Hall was a truly rewarding experience. The guests were two workers who shared their experiences in working their way up to where they currently stand now, both working as QA Analysts at Accenture. They shared valuable insights and tips with us college of computer studies students on navigating the challenges of college and entering the workforce.

One of the workers emphasized the importance of networking and building relationships with mentors in the industry. She shared the value of seeking out opportunities to learn from those who have more experience and can offer guidance and support.

Overall, the lecture was a truly inspiring and informative experience. It was great to hear firsthand accounts of the challenges and triumphs these workers experienced on their journey to where they are now, and the advice they shared will undoubtedly be helpful once we navigate our own paths in the working world.

Freshmen and Shiftee Orientation

On September 15, I attended the freshmen and shiftee orientation for the college of computer studies which became an insightful experience. We were introduced to the faculty and staff of the college, as well as being informed of the rules, guidelines, and other important details related to our studies.

The orientation provided a thorough overview of what to expect during our time in the college, including information on the curriculum, resources available to us, and support services. SU Campus Tour

Attending a tour around the campus on November 9 hosted by one of the campus ambassadors of Silliman University was a wonderful experience. The tour guide was knowledgeable and passionate about the history of the campus, and shared interesting stories and facts about many of the buildings we visited.

One of the highlights of the tour was learning about the history of many buildings that were built before WWII occurred, and has now undergone several renovations. The tour guide also shared information about the various facilities and resources available in each building as well as some fun facts that I didn't know about yet in Silliman.

Overall, the tour was a great way to get to know the campus and learn about the rich history of Silliman University. It was a valuable experience that gave us a sense of belonging and helped us feel more at home on campus.

GitHub Universe 2022

I attended the GitHub Universe 2022 conference virtually on November 10. The conference brought together experts in the field of IT from all over the world, and featured presentations on a wide range of topics including my favorite topic, machine learning.

One of the highlights of the conference was the keynote address by the CEO of GitHub, who shared insights on the future of technology and how it is changing the way we work and collaborate. There were also several breakout sessions where attendees could choose to learn about specific areas of interest in more depth. I personally chose machine-learning and although I was not able to understand that much, I was able to get motivated seeing the advancements of the field.

Overall, the conference was a great opportunity to learn about the latest advancements in the field of IT and to connect with other professionals in the industry. It was an inspiring and informative experience that left me feeling excited and motivated to continue learning and growing in my career.

The Butterfly Project

Watching the live webinar called "The Butterfly Project" on September 24 was a truly thought-provoking and enriching experience. The webinar, hosted by the SUROTC unit, featured a presentation by Al Khaizer B Warad, who emphasized the vulnerability of masculinity and promoted the unity of men and women being equal.

One of the highlights of the webinar was the way it challenged traditional notions of masculinity and explored the ways in which toxic masculinity can be harmful to men and those around them. Warad's discussion was insightful and engaging, and I found myself deeply moved by the message of the webinar.

Overall, watching the "The Butterfly Project" webinar was a truly enriching and valuable experience. It provided valuable insights and challenged me to think more deeply about important issues related to masculinity and gender equality.
