church entries

Christian faith is foundational to Silliman education. Various activities for faith nurture, education, worship, fellowship, and service are in store for Silliman students all year round. These are activities that are receptive to the changing needs and preferences of students. Church activities are designed and organized in a way that does not discriminate students from other religious denominations.

Opening Devotion

On August 22, I had the opportunity to attend the opening devotion for our college of computer studies to kick off the celebration of the university's founding anniversary. It was a special and meaningful event, as it marked the beginning of a week-long series of activities and events to honor the history and legacy of our university.

The devotion was held in the ATA hall and was attended by CCS students together with the Confed. The atmosphere was filled with excitement and a sense of community as we came together to seek a moment with God before we start the celebrations. The program included a prayer, a singing of the national anthem, and a few inspirational words from a pastor.

Overall, it was a faithful and uplifting experience to participate in the opening devotion. I'm grateful to have been a part of this special occasion as it left me looking forward to the rest of the celebrations throughout the week.

University Prayer of Peace

On September 21, I had the opportunity to attend a university prayer of peace organized by the Silliman History and Political Science Society. The purpose of the prayer was to offer prayers for the victims of martial law in the Philippines, and to remind people that we must not forget what happened during that time.

The prayer was held outside the Luce auditorium and was attended by students from different departments. The atmosphere was solemn and respectful as we came together to remember those who suffered and to reaffirm our commitment to peace and justice. The program included a prayer, a moment of silence, and a few words from a few select students.

Overall, it was a moving and meaningful experience to participate in the university prayer of peace. I'm grateful to have been a part of this important event and I'm committed to working towards a more peaceful and just society.

All-University Academic Convocation

On September 28, I had the opportunity to attend the university's academic convocation. It was a prestigious and formal event that marked the beginning of the academic year. The convocation was held in the Luce auditorium and was attended by students, faculty, and staff from all colleges.

The highlight of the program included the words by the university president, who talked about the progress of Silliman and its future road ahead. It was interesting to learn about the various initiatives and projects that the university is working on, and how they contribute to the development of the institution.

Overall, it was a wonderful and informative experience to attend the academic convocation. I'm grateful to have been a part of this special occasion and I'm looking forward to the opportunities and challenges that the new academic year will bring.

Midweek Service

On November 16, I had the opportunity to attend a mass service held by the Silliman Church as part of the university's Christian life emphasis month. It was a special and meaningful event that brought together students, faculty, and staff from all departments to worship and celebrate our faith.

The mass was held in the SU church and was attended by a large crowd of people. The atmosphere was peaceful and spiritual as we sang hymns, listened to the sermon, and participated in the sacraments. The service was led by the pastor and assisted by members of the choir and altar servers.

Overall, it was a wonderful and uplifting experience to attend the mass service. I'm grateful to have been a part of this special occasion and I'm looking forward to participating in more events during the Christian life emphasis month.

Galilean Fellowship

On November 17, I had the opportunity to attend a fellowship called Galilean Fellowship. It was a gathering of students taking the course CHS1, where we were able to get to know more about the book of Samuel. The fellowship was held in front of the ampitheater and SU church and was led by multiple facilitators who guided us through a discussion and reflection on the themes and messages of the book.

I was a lead performer of my group and I was able to initiate and lead my groupmates to be able to successfully provide an excellent output required for our group project. It was a challenging but rewarding experience, as I had to make sure that everyone was on track and that our project met the required standards.

Overall, it was a meaningful and enriching experience to attend the Galilean Fellowship. I'm grateful to have been a part of this special event as it further increased not only my faith but also my leadership abilities.
