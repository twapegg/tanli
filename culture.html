culture entries

Every semester, the Cultural Affairs Committee stages five shows that put value on the appreciation for the arts at what most consider as the “cultural center of the south”: the Claire Isabel McGill Luce Auditorium. Silliman students get a taste of the works of nationally- and internationally-acclaimed literary, visual and performing artists throughout the school year. Season tickets at subsidized rates are made available to students.

Parada Sillimaniana

On the morning of August 22nd, I arrived at the university campus ready to participate in the celebration of the school's founding anniversary. As I made my way to the grandstand seats, I could feel the excitement in the air. When the parade began, each college presented a dance, adding to the lively atmosphere. The floats and marching bands added to the festive atmosphere, and I was thoroughly enjoying myself.

As the parade progressed, I was struck by the sense of community and unity on display. Students from all different majors and backgrounds came together to celebrate this momentous occasion. The grand finale dance performance by all of the colleges combined was a particularly highlight for me, as the vibe and enthusiasm brought the celebration to a goosebumps-giving climax.

Overall, attending the parade in celebration of the university's founding anniversary was a truly memorable experience. The lively atmosphere, diverse array of performances, and sense of community all contributed to a truly festive occasion. I left the parade feeling proud to be a part of this special community and looking forward to many more celebrations to come.
