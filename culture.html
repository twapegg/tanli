culture entries

Every semester, the Cultural Affairs Committee stages five shows that put value on the appreciation for the arts at what most consider as the “cultural center of the south”: the Claire Isabel McGill Luce Auditorium. Silliman students get a taste of the works of nationally- and internationally-acclaimed literary, visual and performing artists throughout the school year. Season tickets at subsidized rates are made available to students.

Movie Night

Last August 22, my friends and I had a movie night together with the college of computer studies. We watched "Everything, Everywhere, All at Once," a absurdist comedy-drama film. The plot centers on a chinese family who runs a laundromat, the mother discovers she must connect with parallel universe versions of herself to prevent a powerful being from destroying the multiverse.

The film was a swirling mix of genre anarchy, with elements of black comedy, science fiction, fantasy, martial arts, and animation. The special effects were impressive, and the acting was superb. I did not expect the movie to have such a sad ending, due to the comedic theme. The movie left me pondering about its beautiful quotes and messages about relationships.

Overall, it was a unique, entertaining, and dramatic film that kept us engaged and laughing throughout. After the movie ended, I was left with sadness because of its message on love and loyalty. Nonetheless, it was a great night spent with good friends and a fantastic film.

Demokra-Sine

On September 21, I had the opportunity to attend a film showing of "Dekada '70," which was hosted by the Silliman History and Political Science Society. It was a powerful and poignant portrayal of a Filipino family during the martial law era in the Philippines. The film follows the story of Amanda Bartolome, a mother of five, who witnesses the tumultuous events of the time through the eyes of her family as they struggle to survive and fight for their rights and freedoms.

The film shed light on the important historical events and social issues of the time, such as student activism, government corruption, and gender inequality. It was a thought-provoking and eye-opening reminder of the struggles and sacrifices of those who fought for democracy and human rights during this painful period in Philippine history.

Overall, it was a deeply moving and impactful film that left a lasting impression on me. I am grateful for the opportunity to have seen it and for the chance to learn more about this important chapter in Philippine history.

Ariniego Art Gallery Tour

Recently on December 13, I had the opportunity to visit the Ariniego Art Gallery, which features the personal collection of Dr. Ariniego as well as artworks by arts students. It was a really enjoyable and enriching experience, and I was able to see a wide variety of crafts and creative artworks.

One of the highlights of the visit for me was getting to see the personal collection of Dr. Ariniego. It was interesting to learn about his background and see the various artworks that he had collected over the years.

I was also really impressed by the artworks of the arts students. It was great to see the creativity and talent of these young artists, and I was really inspired by their work.

Overall, the visit to the Ariniego Art Gallery was a really enjoyable and informative experience. I learned a lot about the different artworks and crafts on display, and I was able to appreciate the talent and creativity of the artists. I would definitely recommend a visit to this art gallery to anyone who loves art and culture.
